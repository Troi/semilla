Semilla stands on four pillars. Each solves a specific problem that kills other intentional communities. No buzzwords, no ideology. Just mechanisms that work.

## Governance

**Problem:** Most communities collapse on governance. Either it's dictatorship (one person decides, others leave), or anarchy (nobody decides, nothing happens), or tyranny of the majority (51% votes = 100% power).

**Solution:** Four-chamber system inspired by historical governance structures that survived centuries.

**Four chambers:**

1. **Chamber of legitimacy** – All member-shareholders, one vote. Decides on basic rules, accepting new members, structural changes. 2/3 majority for fundamental changes. Ensures nobody can be outvoted without broad consensus.

2. **Chamber of taxes** – Voting power proportional to capital contribution. Decides on budget, investments, financial commitments. Ensures those who pay have a say in how money is spent.

3. **Chamber of defense** – Voting power proportional to physical presence (days spent on farm in last year). Decides on security, conflict situations, crisis management. Ensures those physically present have a say in what happens on site.

4. **Chamber of children** – Voting power proportional to number of children in community. Decides on education, long-term investments, sustainability. Ensures community thinks about future, not just present.

**How it works:**
- Each decision must pass through relevant chamber(s)
- Budget must pass chamber of taxes (those who pay) and chamber of legitimacy (everyone agrees)
- Security measures must pass chamber of defense (those on site) and chamber of legitimacy
- Long-term investments must pass chamber of taxes (finance) and chamber of children (future)

**Why it works:**
- Balances different interests – capital, physical presence, legitimacy, future
- Prevents tyranny of majority – no chamber has absolute power
- Motivates right behavior – want influence? Invest capital, time, or children
- Eliminates free riders – if you don't bring value, you don't have voice

## Locality

**Problem:** Global supply chains are fragile. Covid showed how quickly international trade can collapse. Energy crisis showed how dependence on imports kills economies. Food security isn't luxury, it's utility.

**Solution:** Food self-sufficiency as primary goal. Healthcare infrastructure as secondary goal.

**Food self-sufficiency:**
- Vegetables and fruit – year-round production thanks to Uruguayan climate
- Eggs – poultry as most efficient protein source
- Dairy products – goats or sheep (lower requirements than cows)
- Grains – wheat or corn for basic self-sufficiency
- Canning – processing surpluses, winter supplies
- Seeds – own seed production, independence from suppliers

**Healthcare infrastructure:**
- Phase 1: First aid kit, basic equipment, telemedicine
- Phase 2: Clinic, general practitioner or nurse in community
- Phase 3: Small practice, basic surgical procedures, birth assistance
- Goal: Handle 80% of health situations without trip to city

**Why it works:**
- Reduces dependence on external systems
- Provides real value even in BAU scenario (quality food, healthcare)
- In collapse scenario it's difference between survival and catastrophe
- Builds community skill set – agronomist, doctor, craftsman are critical

## Ownership

**Problem:** Most communities fragment ownership. Everyone has piece of land, everyone has their house, everyone has their rules. Result: nobody can leave (property can't be sold), nobody can join (no space), governance is impossible (everyone is king on their own).

**Solution:** Centralized ownership through Uruguayan nominative SA. All land, buildings, infrastructure owned by SA. Member-shareholders own SA shares, not pieces of land.

**Structure:**
- Uruguayan SA (Sociedad Anónima) – standard business structure
- Nominative SA – shares are registered, not bearer (you know who owns)
- Member-shareholders own shares proportional to investment
- SA owns all property – land, buildings, machines, supplies

**Advantages:**
- **Simple exit** – sell shares, not real estate. No building permits, no cadastres, no months of waiting
- **Flexible governance** – SA has bylaws, bylaws define rules. Change bylaws = change rules
- **Legal protection** – SA is legal entity, protects member-shareholders from personal liability
- **Tax efficiency** – Uruguay has no tax on worldwide income for non-residents, SA pays taxes only on Uruguayan income

**Member contract beyond SA:**
- SA bylaws define ownership and basic rules
- Member contract defines rights and obligations of member-shareholders
- Baseline guarantees – what each member-shareholder gets minimum (access to production, residency, exit conditions)
- Opt-in entities – health fund, educational cooperative (voluntary, not mandatory)

**Why it works:**
- Exit is defined and executable – you're not trapped
- Governance is flexible – rules can change as community grows
- Legal structure is standard – Uruguayan lawyers know it, it's not experiment
- Centralized ownership enables efficient management – one owner, clear rules

## Hedge

**Problem:** Most people don't address risk until it's too late. Then it's too late. A good hedge has positive value even if never used.

**Solution:** Residency + functioning farm + community that knows you.

**Residency:**
- Uruguayan residency available with investment or proof of income
- Path to permanent residence (after 3-5 years)
- Possibility to obtain citizenship (after 3-5 years residency)
- Legal stay outside EU/USA – geopolitical diversification

**Functioning farm:**
- Produces food even when you're not there
- Generates income (selling surpluses)
- Maintains value (land + infrastructure)
- Works in BAU and collapse scenario

**Community that knows you:**
- You're not stranger who arrived in crisis
- You're member-shareholder who invested years ahead
- Community knows you, trusts you, counts on you
- In crisis situation it's difference between "who are you?" and "welcome home"

**Why it works:**
- Residency is legal – you're not refugee, you're resident
- Farm works without you – it's not dependent on your physical presence
- Community is real – they're not strangers, they're people you've built project with for years
- Hedge has value even in BAU – residency is optionality, farm is investment, community is network

## How it holds together

Four pillars mutually reinforce:

- **Governance** ensures decisions are legitimate and balanced
- **Locality** ensures community has real value (food, health)
- **Ownership** ensures exit is possible and governance is flexible
- **Hedge** ensures investment makes sense even if collapse doesn't come

Remove one pillar and structure collapses. Governance without locality is empty debate. Locality without ownership is imprisonment. Ownership without governance is chaos. Hedge without all three is illusion.

Semilla isn't perfect. It's a working model that will evolve. But it's built on principles that work for centuries: balanced power, real production, clear ownership, and hedge that has value even if unused.
