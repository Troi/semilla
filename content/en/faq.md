## Isn't this prepper nonsense?

No. Prepper projects are built on fear and isolation. Semilla is built on rational risk analysis and asymmetric bet.

In BAU scenario (business as usual) you have functioning farm producing food, residency in country with pleasant climate, and community of people with whom you share values. That's positive outcome in itself. It's not dependent on collapse.

Prepper buys bunker and canned food and hopes never to need it. Semilla investor has farm that produces food every day, residency that enables spending winter in pleasant climate, and community that works even if collapse doesn't come.

Difference: prepper bets on collapse. Semilla bets on optionality.

## What if Uruguay collapses too?

It can. No country is immune. But Uruguay has several advantages:

**Geopolitical periphery:** Far from major powers, no strategic resources, no reason for invasion. When Europe or USA collapse, Uruguay is out of reach.

**Small population:** 3.5 million people, low density. When systems collapse, periphery survives longer than centers. Montevideo has 1.3 million, rest is countryside.

**Agricultural potential:** Climate enabling year-round production, sufficient rainfall, quality soil. Food self-sufficiency is realistic.

**Stable democracy:** Since 1985 without military coups. Low corruption by regional standards. It's not perfect, but it's better than most of Latin America.

**No guarantee:** Uruguay can collapse. But probability is lower than in Europe or USA. And if it collapses, you have farm that produces food. That's more than most people will have.

## What if community implodes?

It can. Most intentional communities collapse within 5 years. That's why governance is designed to prevent it.

**Four-chamber system:** Balances legitimacy (everyone), taxes (those who pay), defense (those on site), and children (future). No chamber has absolute power. Prevents tyranny of majority.

**Exit is defined:** You can leave under clear conditions. You're not trapped. If community doesn't work, you sell shares and leave. Payment within 12 months.

**Small size:** 20-25 member-shareholders more manageable than 100+. Everyone knows everyone. Conflicts resolved before they escalate.

**Capitalist correctness:** No social obligations beyond explicitly agreed minimum. If you don't bring value, you don't have voice. Free riders eliminated by governance structure.

**No guarantee:** Community can implode. But structure is designed to make it less likely than other communities. And if it implodes, you have exit.

## What if I need money back?

Exit is defined in structure from the start.

**Voluntary exit:**
- Announce intent to leave (in writing, 3 months notice)
- SA has shares valued by independent appraiser (Uruguayan valuer)
- SA has right of first refusal – can buy shares at appraised price
- If SA has no capital, you can sell to third party (with prior consent of chamber of legitimacy)
- Payment within 12 months

**It's not immediate:** SA needs time to raise capital. If you expect money back in a week, Semilla isn't for you.

**It's not guaranteed price:** Shares valued at fair market value. If farm doesn't work, value is low. If it works, value is high. Risk is part of investment.

**It's not liquid asset:** This isn't stock on exchange. It's share in physical asset with long horizon. If you need liquidity, invest elsewhere.

## Why not gold bars / crypto / real estate in CZ?

All three are legitimate strategies. Semilla isn't alternative – it's complement.

**Gold bars / crypto:**
- Advantage: Protection against inflation, portable, independent of state
- Disadvantage: No yield, can't buy food directly, crypto non-functional without internet
- Semilla: Produces food, works without internet, but not portable

**Real estate in CZ:**
- Advantage: Stable asset, rental possibility, you know environment
- Disadvantage: CZ in geopolitical risk zone, real estate non-portable, taxes may rise
- Semilla: Geopolitical periphery, residency enables exit, but you're far from home

**Diversified portfolio:**
- Advantage: Standard strategy, reasonable returns in BAU
- Disadvantage: Financial system can collapse, portfolio inaccessible in crisis
- Semilla: Physical asset outside financial system, but lower liquidity

**Conclusion:** Best strategy is combination. Gold/crypto for portability, real estate in CZ for BAU, diversified portfolio for returns, Semilla for geopolitical diversification and food self-sufficiency. Read [scenarios](/en/scenarios/) for detailed comparison.

## Is it legal?

Yes. Uruguayan law allows land ownership by foreigners.

**Nominative SA:** Standard business structure in Uruguay. Equivalent of Czech a.s. or American Inc. Uruguayan lawyers know it, it's not experiment.

**Residency:** Available with investment ($100k+ in real estate or business) or proof of income ($1500+/month). Legal path to permanent residence and citizenship.

**Taxes:** Uruguay has no tax on worldwide income for non-residents. SA pays taxes only on Uruguayan income. It's not tax evasion, it's legal structure.

**Lawyer:** Final structure will be developed with Uruguayan lawyer. This is working model, not legal commitment.

## Can I live there permanently?

Depends on profile.

**Investor:** Not necessarily. You can spend time on farm (vacation, remote work, long-term stay), but it's not obligation. Residency enables legal stay, but you don't have to be there physically.

**Builder:** Yes, but with condition. Minimum 3 months per year (first 3 years). Then depends on agreement with community. If you want to live there permanently, it's possible (if community agrees).

**Consumer:** No. Maximum 2 months per year. No residency, no permanent housing.

**Capacity:** Depends on phase. Phase 1 has temporary housing (containers), capacity 3-5 people. Phase 2 has permanent housing (houses), capacity 10-15 people. Phase 3 has expanded housing, capacity 20-30 people.

## What if I don't get along with someone?

Governance is designed to resolve conflicts, not ignore them.

**Small community:** 20-25 member-shareholders. Everyone knows everyone. Conflicts resolved before they escalate.

**Exit is defined:** If you don't get along with someone and community doesn't resolve it, you can leave. You're not trapped.

**Expulsion is possible:** If someone seriously violates rules (theft, violence, repeated governance ignoring), chamber of legitimacy can vote on expulsion (2/3 majority).

**No guarantee:** People are people. Conflicts will happen. But structure is designed to resolve them, not ignore them.

## What if you die?

Governance isn't dependent on one person.

**SA has bylaws:** Rules work without founder. Chambers decide, not individual.

**Member-shareholders own shares:** If I die, my shares pass to heirs (wife, children). They decide whether to continue or sell.

**Community continues:** SA isn't dependent on me. If I built well, community works without me.

**Risk:** Phase 0-1 more dependent on founder than Phase 2-3. If I die in Phase 0, project probably fails. If I die in Phase 3, community continues.
