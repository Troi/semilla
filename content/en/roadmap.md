Semilla isn't a finished project. It's a prototype that will evolve based on who joins and what they bring. The roadmap below is a working plan, not a commitment. Numbers are estimates, not guarantees.

## Phase 0: Prototype (2026)

**Goal:** Validate concept, find first co-founders, explore Uruguay.

**Activities:**
- Complete website and basic communication
- Exploratory trip to Uruguay (2-3 weeks)
  - Tour regions (Canelones, Maldonado, Rocha)
  - Meet with real estate agents and lawyers
  - Understand local market and legal environment
- Find 2-3 co-founders (investor or builder profile)
- Develop legal structure with Uruguayan lawyer
- Create financial model (costs, cash flow, exit scenarios)

**Costs:** $5k-$10k (trip, lawyer, basic due diligence)

**Trigger point for Phase 1:** Minimum 3 co-founders + $150k capital + legal structure ready

## Phase 1: Foundation (2027)

**Goal:** Buy land, establish SA, obtain residency for founders.

**Activities:**
- Establish Uruguayan nominative SA
- Purchase land (50-100 hectares)
  - Region: Canelones or Maldonado (near Montevideo, good climate)
  - Criteria: water access, quality soil, electricity, road
- Residency for founders (investment or income path)
- Basic infrastructure
  - Well or water connection
  - Solar panels + batteries (off-grid capable)
  - Temporary housing (containers or mobile homes)
- First planting
  - Fruit trees (citrus, avocado, figs)
  - Vegetables (tomatoes, peppers, lettuce)
  - Poultry (chickens for eggs)

**Costs:** $150k-$250k
- Land: $80k-$120k (50-100 ha, $1000-$1500/ha)
- Legal and residency: $20k-$30k
- Basic infrastructure: $30k-$50k
- Planting and equipment: $20k-$50k

**Trigger point for Phase 2:** Functioning farm (vegetable and egg production) + minimum 5 member-shareholders + $300k capital

## Phase 2: Functioning farm (2028–2029)

**Goal:** Food self-sufficiency for 5-10 people, permanent housing, community expansion.

**Activities:**
- Permanent housing
  - 2-3 houses (timber frame or adobe)
  - Common spaces (kitchen, dining room, workspace)
  - Sanitary facilities (bathrooms, laundry)
- Production expansion
  - Greenhouse for year-round production
  - Goats or sheep (milk, cheese)
  - Fruit orchard expansion
  - Grains (wheat or corn)
- Processing and storage
  - Canning (preserving, drying)
  - Cooling (refrigerator, freezer on solar power)
  - Storage (dry foods, seeds, tools)
- Accept additional member-shareholders (total 8-12)
  - Priority: agronomist, doctor/nurse, craftsman

**Costs:** $300k-$500k
- Housing: $150k-$250k (3 houses + common spaces)
- Production: $80k-$120k (greenhouse, animals, machines)
- Processing: $40k-$60k (canning, cooling, storage)
- Operating capital: $30k-$70k (seeds, feed, maintenance)

**Trigger point for Phase 3:** Food self-sufficiency proven (12 months without buying basic foods) + minimum 10 member-shareholders + doctor or nurse in community

## Phase 3: Clinic and expansion (2030+)

**Goal:** Basic healthcare, children's education, expansion to 15-25 member-shareholders.

**Activities:**
- Healthcare infrastructure
  - Clinic (practice, first aid kit, basic equipment)
  - General practitioner or nurse full-time
  - Telemedicine (consultations with specialists)
  - Basic surgical procedures (stitches, fractures)
- Education
  - School for children (if 5+)
  - Teacher or homeschooling cooperative
  - Library and educational materials
- Community expansion
  - Additional housing (total 6-8 houses)
  - Production expansion (more animals, larger orchards)
  - Possible diversification (bees, fish, mushrooms)
- Opt-in entities
  - Health fund (shared healthcare financing)
  - Educational cooperative (shared school financing)

**Costs:** $400k-$700k
- Clinic: $100k-$150k (building, equipment, doctor salary)
- School: $50k-$100k (building, materials, teacher salary)
- Housing: $200k-$350k (additional 3-5 houses)
- Production expansion: $50k-$100k

**Trigger point for next phases:** Community decides. Options: expansion to 50+ people, diversification to other regions, or stabilization at 20-25 member-shareholders.

## Risks and uncertainties

**Political risk:** Uruguay is stable, but politics can change. Residency rules may tighten. Taxes may rise. Solution: diversification (not all member-shareholders in UY), legal structure prepared for changes.

**Economic risk:** Uruguayan peso is volatile. Inflation may rise. Costs may be higher than estimate. Solution: 30% capital reserve, prices in USD, production reduces market dependence.

**Community risk:** People leave, conflicts, governance fails. Solution: exit is defined, governance designed to balance interests, small community (20-25) more manageable than large.

**Climate risk:** Drought, floods, frosts. Solution: production diversification, reserves, insurance (if available).

**Personal risk:** Founder (me) may die, get sick, or change mind. Solution: governance not dependent on one person, SA has bylaws that work without founder.

## What this means for you

**If you're an investor:**
- Phase 0-1: High risk, no production, no residency (yet). But lowest entry price.
- Phase 2: Medium risk, functioning farm, residency available. Higher entry price.
- Phase 3: Low risk, proven self-sufficiency, healthcare. Highest entry price.

**If you're a builder:**
- Phase 0-1: Maximum influence, building from scratch, low competition. But most work.
- Phase 2: Still great influence, community forming, infrastructure growing. Still plenty of work.
- Phase 3: Less influence (more people = smaller vote share), but more stable environment. Less work.

**If you're a consumer:**
- Phase 0-1: Not available (no production yet).
- Phase 2: Possible but limited (small production).
- Phase 3: Ideal (surpluses for sale).
