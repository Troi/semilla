## Future matrix

Nobody knows the future. A good strategy works across multiple scenarios. The table below shows how different investment strategies perform in different future scenarios.

**Legend:**
- 🟢 Good – strategy works, you achieve your goals
- 🟡 You'll survive – not ideal position, but you'll manage
- 🟠 Problem – significant losses or complications
- 🔴 Catastrophe – strategy fails, major impacts

## Scenarios (rows)

**BAU (Business As Usual)** – Slow demographic decline, economies grow slower but systems hold. States keep borrowing, but defaults don't come. Inflation under control. Geopolitical stability disrupted but without major conflicts.

**Soft recession 2030s** – AI bubble bursts, fiscal correction in EU and USA, unemployment rises. States cut spending, pension systems reform. Social tension grows but institutions hold. Real estate drops 20-30%, stocks volatile.

**Hard collapse 2035–2045** – Cascading state defaults (starting with EU periphery), eurozone breakup, geopolitical instability. Banking system in crisis, capital controls. Migration from affected areas. Food and energy security a priority.

**Black swan** – Major conflict (Taiwan, Ukraine escalation, Middle East), rapid breakdown of international trade. Supply chains collapse, inflation 20%+, states impose war economy. Rapid destabilization within 1-2 years.

## Strategies (columns)

**Do nothing** – Keep savings in bank, no active steps, rely on the state.

**Gold bars / crypto** – Physical gold or Bitcoin as protection against inflation and fiat currency collapse.

**Real estate in Czech Republic** – Investment in Czech real estate as capital protection.

**Diversified portfolio** – Stocks, bonds, ETFs, real estate, commodities – classic financial diversification.

**Semilla – investor** – Capital entry into Semilla, passive participation, share of production, residency as hedge.

**Semilla – builder** – Active membership, skill contribution, lower capital entry, physical presence in community.

## Rating explanations

### Do nothing
- **BAU:** 🟡 Inflation slowly erodes value, but you'll survive.
- **Soft recession:** 🟠 Savings lose value, state support declines.
- **Hard collapse:** 🔴 Banking crisis, capital controls, savings disappear.
- **Black swan:** 🔴 Total loss of access to money, hyperinflation.

### Gold bars / crypto
- **BAU:** 🟡 Protection against inflation, but no yield, opportunity cost.
- **Soft recession:** 🟢 Value rises when trust in fiat declines.
- **Hard collapse:** 🟡 Gold works, crypto volatile, but can't buy food directly.
- **Black swan:** 🟠 Gold hard to exchange, crypto non-functional without internet.

### Real estate in CZ
- **BAU:** 🟢 Stable asset, rental possibility, capital protection.
- **Soft recession:** 🟡 Prices drop, but you hold the asset, rental works.
- **Hard collapse:** 🟠 Taxes rise, law enforcement declines, possible confiscation.
- **Black swan:** 🔴 CZ in geopolitical risk zone, real estate non-portable.

### Diversified portfolio
- **BAU:** 🟢 Standard strategy works, reasonable returns.
- **Soft recession:** 🟡 Volatility, but diversification protects from total loss.
- **Hard collapse:** 🟠 Stocks collapse, bonds default, gold helps but not enough.
- **Black swan:** 🔴 Financial system doesn't work, portfolio inaccessible.

### Semilla – investor
- **BAU:** 🟢 Functioning farm, residency, vacation, share of production.
- **Soft recession:** 🟢 Physical asset outside financial system, food production stable.
- **Hard collapse:** 🟢 Residency works, farm produces, exit from risk zone.
- **Black swan:** 🟡 Uruguay relatively safe, but logistics complex, passive role limits adaptability.

### Semilla – builder
- **BAU:** 🟢 Active community, skill utilization, quality of life.
- **Soft recession:** 🟢 Physical presence, food production, independence from financial system.
- **Hard collapse:** 🟢 Full integration in community, governance works, self-sufficiency.
- **Black swan:** 🟢 You're on site, you have skills, community knows you, maximum resilience.

## Conclusion

Semilla isn't the best strategy in BAU scenario – diversified portfolio or Czech real estate have better risk/reward. But Semilla is the only strategy that works well in **all** scenarios. That's the definition of a hedge.

If you believe BAU will continue forever, Semilla isn't for you. If you acknowledge the future is uncertain and want a strategy that works even when you're wrong, Semilla makes sense.
