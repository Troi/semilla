About

My name is Vít Fux. I'm 40 years old, software developer, living in South Moravia with my wife and two small children. Semilla isn't my business – it's a hedge for my family and for people who see the same risk.

How I got here

It started with demography. Europe has fertility of 1.5, needs 2.1. Each generation is a third smaller. Pension systems are built on population growth. When growth ends, the system collapses. It's not theory, it's arithmetic.

Then fiscal policy. State debts grow faster than economies. Interest payments consume ever-larger share of budgets. Politicians promise more than they can deliver. When trust disappears, the system collapses quickly. History shows civilizations don't fall linearly – they hold, hold, then collapse within a decade.

Then geopolitics. Ukraine showed how quickly security situation can change. Energy crisis showed how fragile supply chains are. Covid showed how quickly international trade can collapse. It's not paranoia, it's observation.

I don't want to be a prepper. I don't want bunker and canned food. I want an asymmetric bet that works even if I'm wrong. If collapse doesn't come, I have farm and residency in pleasant country. If it comes, I have somewhere to go and something to eat. That's the definition of hedge.

Why Uruguay

Geopolitical periphery. Far from Europe, Russia, China. No strategic resources worth invading for. Stable democracy since 1985. Low corruption by regional standards.

Climate enabling year-round production. Mild winters, warm summers, sufficient rainfall. Agricultural potential without dependence on irrigation. You can grow food all year.

Legal system allowing land ownership by foreigners. Residency available with investment or proof of income. No taxes on worldwide income for non-residents. It's not tax haven, but it's reasonable.

Small population (3.5 million), low density, plenty of space. When systems collapse, periphery survives longer than centers. Uruguay isn't perfect, but it's the best choice I found.

Why now

Time window 2025–2035. Before anticipated recession, after covid (land prices stabilized), before next wave of inflation. The longer you wait, the more expensive it will be. The more people see the same problem, the greater the competition for same solutions.

I have two small children. I don't want to leave them debt and collapsing system. I want to leave them optionality. Possibility to live in Europe if it works, possibility to leave if it doesn't. That's parent's responsibility.

Best time to plant tree was twenty years ago. Second best is now.

What I'm looking for

People who see it the same way. Who understand demography, fiscal policy, geopolitics. Who don't need apocalyptic rhetoric to understand that systems fail.

People who bring more than money. Doctors, agronomists, craftsmen, developers, teachers. People who have skills the community needs.

People who understand that best hedge is one you never use. That BAU use case must work on its own. That this isn't escape, it's asymmetric bet.

People who are willing to invest time, not just money. Who understand that community works on trust, not contracts. Who are willing to build something long-term.

What I'm not offering

I'm not offering certainty. Semilla is prototype, not finished project. It can fail. Uruguay can collapse. Community can implode. Governance can not work. These are risks.

I'm not offering quick profit. This isn't startup investment. It's physical asset with long horizon. If you want 10x return in 3 years, don't look for Semilla.

I'm not offering social experiment. Governance is designed to work, not to be ideologically pure. Four-chamber system balances legitimacy, taxes, defense, and children. If that sounds complicated, read structure.

I'm not offering escape. If only reason you're considering Semilla is fear of collapse, you're not ready. Hedge works only when you don't need it.

Where I am now

Phase 0, beginning. Website exists. Exploratory trip to Uruguay planned for Q2 2026. Looking for 2-3 co-founders. If this makes sense, write to me.

I'm not salesman. I'm not guru. I'm developer with two children who sees risk and wants to address it rationally. If you see it the same way, let's talk.
