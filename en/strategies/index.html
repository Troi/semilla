For whom

Semilla isn't for everyone. It's for people who see the same risk, have capital or relevant skills, and want an asymmetric bet that works even when they're wrong. Three profiles, three different ways to participate.

Investor

What you bring: Capital. Minimum $50k USD, ideally $100k+. No active involvement required.

What you get:

  • Share in Uruguayan SA proportional to investment
  • Share of SA profit (dividend) proportional to your stake; net payouts after reasonable administrative costs. Note: you bear operational and market risk — lower profit or loss are possible.
  • Residency – legal stay in Uruguay, path to permanent residence
  • Possibility to spend time on the farm (vacation, remote work, long-term stay)
  • Exit defined in contract – you know under what conditions you can leave and what you get back
  • Hedge – when systems collapse, you have somewhere to go and something to eat

What you don't do:

  • Don't have to move
  • Don't have to actively work on the farm
  • Don't have to participate in governance (but you can)
  • Don't have to be physically present

For whom:

  • You have capital and want to diversify it outside the financial system
  • You see demographic and fiscal risk but don't want to change lifestyle
  • You want residency as optionality for yourself or family
  • You understand that the best hedge is one you never use

Builder

What you bring: Skills. Doctor, agronomist, craftsman, software developer, teacher, nurse. People who can do things the community needs.

What you get:

  • Place in cooperative (coop) – membership primarily based on skill contribution; capital entry may be lower ($20k–$30k) and complemented by work
  • Direct access to governance – a vote in decision-making of the cooperative and the community
  • Share of production and goods at cost price
  • Possibility to use your skills in the community (paid work or barter)
  • Priority access to infrastructure (housing, healthcare, children's education)
  • Residency – legal stay in Uruguay
  • Hedge + community – you're not alone

What you do:

  • Pay a regular subscription (membership) fee for participation in the cooperative
  • Physical presence minimum 3 months per year (first 3 years)
  • Active participation in governance – voting, proposals, discussions
  • Skill contribution – using your skillset for the community
  • Building relationships – community works on trust, not contracts

For whom:

  • You have skills the community needs
  • You want to actively build something meaningful
  • You're willing to invest time, not just money
  • You understand that the best hedge is a community that knows you

Consumer

What you bring: Smaller capital ($10k-$15k). You don't want residency, don't want governance, just want access to production and optionality.

What you get:

  • Smaller share in SA – no voice in governance, no residency
  • Share of farm production at cost price (vegetables, fruit, eggs)
  • Possibility to spend time on the farm (vacation, remote work, max 2 months per year)
  • Possibility to upgrade to investor or builder later

What you don't do:

  • Don't have voice in governance
  • Don't have residency
  • Don't have priority access to infrastructure
  • Don't have guaranteed exit – you're last in line during liquidation

For whom:

  • You want to try the concept before investing more
  • You're interested in quality food production, not hedge
  • You don't want to move or actively build
  • You understand you get less because you bring less

How to decide

You're an investor if:

  • You have capital ($50k+)
  • You want residency as hedge
  • You don't want to change lifestyle
  • You see Semilla as part of diversified portfolio

You're a builder if:

  • You have skills the community needs
  • You want to actively build community
  • You're willing to invest time and physical presence
  • You see Semilla as long-term life project

You're a consumer if:

  • You want to try the concept with lower risk
  • You're interested in production, not governance
  • You don't want residency or active participation
  • You might upgrade to investor or builder later

What Semilla isn't

It's not charity. If you don't have capital or skills, you can't participate. Community works because everyone brings value.

It's not a social experiment. Governance is designed to work, not to be ideologically pure. Four-chamber system balances legitimacy, taxes, defense, and children. If that sounds complicated, read structure.

It's not an escape. BAU use case must work on its own. If the only reason you're considering Semilla is fear of collapse, you're not ready. Hedge works only when you don't need it.

It's not for free riders. Free riders kill communities. Exit is defined, but not easy. If you plan to take money and disappear, don't look for Semilla.
